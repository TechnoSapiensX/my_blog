I spent the better part of a decade being the person who kept systems running — SRE at Cohesity, Linux admin at EOS, operations at Sify Technologies. I was good at it. But I kept noticing the decisions I wanted to influence were being made in rooms I wasn't in.

So I did the MBA. Not because I had a crisis — because I had a direction and needed the tools to get there. The honest version of that year? Half was learning new frameworks. The other half was learning to see myself clearly — the parts that work, and the parts that quietly get in the way.

My Insights Discovery profile calls me a Reforming Director — Fiery Red dominant, results-driven, impatient with inefficiency. Accurate. What I didn't expect was how useful it is to have those patterns named precisely. It's hard to change what you can't see.

Where I'm headed

Senior cyber security leadership — CISO-track roles, AI governance, or strategy consulting at the intersection of technology and business risk. I want to be the person in that room now, making those decisions well.

Currently working through CISSP certification and AI governance programmes, posting on LinkedIn about cyber leadership, and looking for the right post-MBA opportunity.
